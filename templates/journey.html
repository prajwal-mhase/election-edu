The Election Journey 🗳️

From the announcement of elections to the declaration of results — follow every step of India's democratic process.

Step 1

Election Schedule Announced

The Election Commission of India (ECI) announces the election schedule. This instantly triggers the Model Code of Conduct (MCC), restricting government spending and promises during the election period.

📋 MCC enforced from day 1
📢
📝
Step 2

Nomination of Candidates

Anyone wishing to contest must file a nomination form with the Returning Officer. A security deposit is required (₹25,000 for Lok Sabha). Nominations are then scrutinized for validity.

🔍 Scrutiny follows filing
Step 3

Withdrawal of Candidates

After scrutiny, candidates have a window to withdraw their nomination without penalty. After this period, the final list of candidates is published and symbols are allotted.

🏛️ Symbols assigned post-withdrawal
✋
📣
Step 4

Election Campaign

Candidates and parties campaign across the constituency — rallies, door-to-door visits, advertisements. Campaigning must cease 48 hours before polling day (the "silence period").

🔇 48-hr silence before voting
Step 5

Polling Day

Voters visit their assigned polling booth, verify identity, receive a ballot (digital via EVM), and press the button next to their chosen candidate. VVPAT provides paper trail. Ink is applied to finger to prevent double voting.

🖊️ Indelible ink on finger
🗳️
🔒
Step 6

Sealing & Transportation of EVMs

After polling closes, EVMs are sealed in the presence of candidates' agents. They are transported under tight security to counting centers and stored in strongrooms with multi-layer security.

🛡️ Multi-party seal verification
Step 7

Vote Counting

On counting day, EVMs are opened round-by-round in counting halls. Representatives of all candidates monitor each table. Postal ballots (for service voters) are counted first. Results are updated in real time.

📊 Round-by-round tallying
🔢
🏆
Step 8

Result Declaration

The candidate with the most votes (First-Past-The-Post) in a constituency wins. The Returning Officer issues a certificate of election. The winning party or coalition then forms the government.

🇮🇳 Democracy in action

Voter Registration Guide

Everything you need to register and vote in India
📋

Who Can Register?

  • Indian citizen aged 18+
  • Ordinary resident of India
  • Not of unsound mind
  • Not disqualified by law
📄

Documents Needed

  • Proof of Age (Birth Certificate / 10th Marksheet)
  • Proof of Address (Aadhaar / Utility Bill)
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Aadhaar Card (recommended)
💻

How to Register

  • Online: voters.eci.gov.in
  • Voter Helpline App
  • Form 6 at local ERO office
  • Through Booth Level Officers
📅

Key Deadlines

  • Register well before election date
  • Check electoral roll before each election
  • Update address before moving
  • Corrections via Form 8
